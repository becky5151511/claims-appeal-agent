Member ID: MEM-31504
Previous Plan: Fidelis Care Medicaid Managed Care
New Plan: River Spring Health LIFE (PACE Program)
Transition Effective Date: January 4, 2025

Provider: Metro Home Health Agency
Service Type: Personal Care Aide (HCPCS T1019) — 3 days, 4 hours/day
Service Dates: January 1, 2, and 3, 2025
Billed Amount: $486.00 ($162.00/day)

Background:
Member is a 82-year-old Bronx resident with moderate dementia and mobility 
limitations, requiring daily personal care assistance. Member was enrolled in 
Fidelis Care Medicaid Managed Care with an active authorization for personal care 
aide services (4 hours/day) valid through December 31, 2024.

The member's family initiated a plan transition to River Spring Health LIFE (a PACE 
program) in November 2024. The enrollment application was submitted November 19, 2024. 
River Spring processed the enrollment with an effective date of January 4, 2025.

Fidelis Care coverage terminated December 31, 2024.

This created a 3-day gap (January 1–3, 2025) during which:
- Fidelis Care coverage had ended
- River Spring coverage had not yet begun
- The member's home health aide continued services without interruption, as 
  discontinuing care for a dementia patient with mobility issues posed a safety risk

Fidelis Care denial (separate claim CLM-FC-2024-88821):
Fidelis Care also denied the January 1–3 dates, stating coverage ended December 31, 
2024 and no retroactive extension was authorized.

Both plans have denied the same three days of service.

Key facts:
- River Spring enrollment application was submitted November 19, 2024
- The 3-day gap resulted from River Spring's processing timeline, not member action
- Interrupting personal care services for an 82-year-old dementia patient creates 
  documented safety and health risks
- The home health aide continued services in good faith to ensure member safety
- New York State Medicaid transition-of-care protections may apply
